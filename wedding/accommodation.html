CAPE TOWN

Roné has just been in Cape Town for work and they stayed in the Home From Home apartments ... she stayed in the Harbour Edge one and the prices are pretty reasonable there. They cater for single room, double room, 2 and 3 bedroom apartments.
I've done quite a bit of searching around for good websites for accommodation in Cape Town and am struggling to find ones that bring back good results with all the info you need displayed. The best site that I've come across is Cape Stay.
Cape Stay search site offers the ability to search particular suburbs in CT. Generally, accommodation will be cheaper in the southern and northern suburbs compared with atlantic seaboard and city bowl. It does then mean a drive into the city for some things (roughly 20-30 minutes for southern suburbs and 30-40 minutes for northern suburbs), but many of the attractions and activities are out of the city so I don't particularly feel like one needs to stay there. Stellenbosch and Somerset West are 2 other options - Stellies certainly is very beautiful. But note that they are 50km out of CT and it's quite dangerous to drive the N2 at night. No matter where you stay you will still need a car. I would recommend looking for a spot in southern suburbs if your budget is limited since it's fairly central to everything. See further details below.
Wherever you stay in Cape Town it is advised to keep security features in mind, especially if you're hiring out a house. Things like security gates, electric fencing and alarms provide that extra level of security and peace of mind. It really does depend on the area and the particular accommodation - a secure apartment block vs a house for example. If you're unsure or would like some help deciding then just ask us.
 
Here are the CT suburbs as sectioned by the Cape Stay website, with links to search for accommodation within a particular area (use google maps to see where the individual areas lie in relation to the city and everything else):
 
Southern Suburbs
Of the areas listed I would say to avoid staying in Observatory, Pinelands, Plumstead and Wynberg. Good central options would be Bishopscourt, Claremont, Kenilworth, Newlands, Rosebank and Rondebosch. The areas towards False Bay (Constantia, Tokai etc) are good but not as central.
 
City Bowl
All very nice areas, possibly a bit more pricey but you never know what you might find!
 
Atlantic Seaboard
Also all very nice areas to stay, but make sure you check on google maps where they are - some are close to the city (like Sea Point and Three Anchor Bay) but others are heading out along the peninsula and although would be lovely to stay at they will require more driving to get to things and places (like Misty Cliffs, Scarborough, Kalk Bay and Fish Hoek).
 
Northern Suburbs (Tygerberg)
Of the areas listed I would say to avoid staying in are Parow, Goodwood, Belville and Tygerberg. Good areas to stay are Durbanville and Tygervalley.
Roné is going to Cape Town in April this year (2016) and her athletes are staying in these guest units which look great and good price too!
 
Winelands
All really beautiful areas but again check on google maps where they are - for example Paarl is a 50 minute drive from Cape Town city centre.

KRUGER NATIONAL PARK

On the Itinerary page you'll see we keen to do 3 nights in Kruger at the end of our trip. If you're keen to join then let us know, it would be great to have you along! We will soon finalise and flesh out the details for the Kruger trip because we will need to get our accommodation booked asap as they get booked very far in advance. You can find all information you need for Kruger on the SAN Parks website - camps, gates, day entry, and accommodation and game drive bookings.
If you want to do Kruger at a different time to us, or want to get your bookings done despite what we end up doing, then please just go ahead and get yourselves sorted. Kruger is a very big park so it's important to note what gate you plan to enter in and out from, and to take into account the distances between camps and gates and the speed limit. The How To Get There page on the website has some good info on the approximate travel times between gates and camps. Remember the point is not to speed from one to the other but rather to amble along very slowly while on the lookout for game! So rather keep distances short and allocate lots of time to get around.
Because it is a lot of stop start and very slow driving I would recommend getting an automatic rental car!
